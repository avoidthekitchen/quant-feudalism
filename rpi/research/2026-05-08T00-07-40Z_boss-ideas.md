## Boss System Overview

### When Bosses Appear

Bosses appear every 5 rounds (round 5, 10, 15, ...) **after the player clears all normal enemies** in that round.

### Boss Entry Sequence

1. Last normal enemy dies
2. 1.5s pause — arena darkens, edges pulse
3. Boss spawns at the center of the arena with a dramatic entry animation
4. Extraction gate remains locked until boss dies (mandatory fight)
5. Boss does not spawn if the player retreats before clearing normals (retreat = normal retreat rules, reduced rewards, no boss encounter)

### Boss Round Composition

Boss rounds have a **full normal wave** (same count as non-boss rounds: `min(spawnPositions.length, 5 + roundsFinished)`) followed by the boss. The player must manage resources across both phases.

### HP Scaling

Bosses use the same `1 + 0.06 * roundsFinished` multiplier as normal enemies, applied to a much higher base HP.

### Selection

Boss is randomly selected from a pool. At launch: 3 boss designs. Each boss has unique mechanics that do not exist in the normal enemy roster.

### Rewards

Bosses drop unique rewards (specifics TBD). Boss kills also award a large Bug Bounty Credit bonus.

## Boss 1: Architect

*A stationary construct that reshapes the arena around the player. Controls space with walls, zones, and structural attacks.*

### Concept

The Architect doesn't chase or flee. It sits at the center and reconfigures the arena. The player must navigate an ever-changing battlefield while finding windows to deal damage. The Architect is the only fight where positioning matters more than timing.

### Stats

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Base HP | 280 |
| HP at round 5 | 364 (× 1.30) |
| HP at round 10 | 448 (× 1.60) |
| HP at round 15 | 532 (× 1.90) |
| Touch damage | 20 |
| Movement | None (stationary at center) |
| Hitbox radius | 50px |

### Phase System

The Architect has 2 phases, transitioning at 50% HP.

**Phase 1: Construction**
- Builds walls and hazard zones around the arena
- Fires structural projectiles from its body
- Predictable patterns, moderate threat

**Phase 2: Demolition**
- Walls begin to shatter, sending debris projectiles
- Hazard zones expand
- Attack speed increases
- The arena becomes chaotic — the Architect is destroying what it built

### Attack 1: Raise Wall

The Architect summons a wall segment from the ground.

- **Wall properties:** 200px long, 20px thick, blocks movement and projectiles (both player and enemy)
- **Duration:** 8 seconds, then crumbles over 1 second
- **Cooldown:** 3.0s
- **Max active walls:** 4
- **Placement:** Walls are placed to create corridors and dead ends around the player. The Architect targets the player's current position and places walls to funnel movement
- **Phase 2 change:** Walls crumble early (4s instead of 8s), and each crumbling wall launches 3 debris projectiles in random directions (15 damage each, 200 px/s)

### Attack 2: Compression Zone

The Architect marks a rectangular area on the ground that contracts over time.

- **Zone properties:** Starts at 160px × 160px, contracts to 40px × 40px over 1.5 seconds
- **Damage:** 12 per 0.3s tick while inside the contracting zone
- **Cooldown:** 4.0s
- **Telegraph:** Red outlined rectangle appears 0.5s before contraction starts
- **Phase 2 change:** Two zones spawn simultaneously instead of one

### Attack 3: Pillar Slam

The Architect slams a structural pillar down at the player's position.

- **Telegraph:** Crosshair appears at player's position for 0.6s
- **Impact:** 28 damage in a 80px radius, launches debris ring outward (4 projectiles at 12 damage each, 250 px/s)
- **Cooldown:** 3.5s
- **Phase 2 change:** Two pillars slam in quick succession (0.3s apart), second targets predicted dash position

### Attack 4: Gravity Well (Phase 2 Only)

The Architect creates a persistent gravity pull at a random position in the arena.

- **Radius:** 150px
- **Pull strength:** 120 px/s toward center
- **Duration:** 5 seconds
- **Cooldown:** 8.0s
- **Interaction with player:** Player is affected by the pull but can Dash out. Does not pull the Architect (it's stationary)
- **Synergy with Compression Zone:** Gravity Well can pull the player into an active Compression Zone

### Vulnerability Windows

The Architect is always targetable but has a damage reduction mechanic:

- **Structural Shield:** The Architect has a persistent 30% damage reduction
- **Overload:** After using any attack, the Architect enters a 0.8s "cooldown" where the structural shield drops (0% damage reduction). Brief white flash indicates the window
- **Phase transition:** When transitioning to Phase 2, the Architect is fully vulnerable for 2.0s (no damage reduction) during the demolition animation

### Player Counterplay

- **Read the wall patterns:** Walls funnel movement. The player must anticipate where walls will appear and avoid getting boxed in
- **Punish Overload windows:** Time attacks for the 0.8s vulnerability after each Architect attack
- **Dash through debris:** Phase 2 debris from crumbling walls can be avoided with well-timed Dashes
- **Card synergies:**
  - **Singularity** pulls debris projectiles back toward center (cleanup)
  - **Phase Shift** teleports through walls
  - **Shockwave** pushes debris projectiles away
  - **Precision** rewards staying at range (Architect is stationary, easy to maintain distance)
  - **Convergence Matrix** scaffold: if debris clusters near the Architect, +30% damage to it

### Visual Design

- Large crystalline/geometric construct at arena center
- Walls rise from the ground with a stone/metal texture
- Compression zones have a red shrinking outline
- Phase 2: construct cracks and glows from within, walls flicker and crumble
- Debris is angular rock/metal shards

## Boss 2: Swarm Queen

*A massive central entity that continuously spawns smaller minions. The fight is about managing the swarm while finding openings to damage the Queen herself.*

### Concept

The Swarm Queen is the only fight with continuously spawning enemies. She doesn't attack directly — instead, she produces waves of minion Bugs and larval projectiles while the player must decide between clearing minions and attacking the Queen. This boss tests target prioritization and resource management more than any other encounter.

### Stats

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Base HP | 220 |
| HP at round 5 | 286 (× 1.30) |
| HP at round 10 | 352 (× 1.60) |
| HP at round 15 | 418 (× 1.90) |
| Touch damage | 18 |
| Movement | Slow drift toward player at 40 px/s |
| Hitbox radius | 55px |
| Minion cap | 6 alive at once |

### Phase System

2 phases, transitioning at 50% HP.

**Phase 1: Brooding**
- Spawns standard minion Bugs
- Fires egg projectiles that hatch into minions on impact
- Slow, methodical spawn rate

**Phase 2: Frenzy**
- Spawn rate doubles
- Minions become faster (+30% movement speed)
- Queen gains a burst attack
- Queen stops drifting, stays stationary

### Attack 1: Spawn Brood

The Queen lays an egg that hatches into a minion Bug after a short delay.

- **Egg properties:** Stationary object, 30 HP, hatches after 2.0s
- **Hatched minion:** Standard Bug with 22 HP (half normal), normal chase/lunge AI, 10 touch damage
- **Spawned minions DO NOT count toward round enemy count** — they're bonus enemies created by the boss
- **Spawn cooldown:** 4.0s (Phase 1), 2.0s (Phase 2)
- **Egg placement:** Dropped at Queen's current position

### Attack 2: Egg Launcher

The Queen fires an egg projectile at the player.

- **Projectile speed:** 280 px/s
- **On impact:** Hatches immediately into a minion Bug
- **If egg hits player:** 15 damage + hatches minion at player's position
- **Cooldown:** 5.0s (Phase 1), 3.0s (Phase 2)
- **Telegraph:** 0.4s windup, Queen rears back visibly

### Attack 3: Brood Surge (Phase 2 Only)

The Queen releases a burst of 3 eggs simultaneously in a spread pattern.

- **Eggs:** Same as Egg Launcher but 3 projectiles in a 60-degree cone
- **Cooldown:** 8.0s
- **Telegraph:** 0.6s windup with visible pulsing

### Attack 4: Shriek (Phase 2 Only)

The Queen emits a shockwave that pushes the player away and briefly stuns.

- **Radius:** 200px
- **Effect:** Player knocked back 120px and movement-locked for 0.3s
- **Damage:** 12
- **Cooldown:** 10.0s
- **Telegraph:** 0.5s charge-up with visible energy buildup
- **This is the Queen's defensive tool** — it creates space when the player gets close

### Interaction with Eggs

Eggs (30 HP) can be destroyed before they hatch:

- **Melee kills eggs instantly** (any melee damage one-shots them)
- **Ranged attacks destroy eggs in one hit** (Bolt, any Function card)
- **Destroying an egg does NOT spawn a minion**
- **Singularity** pulls eggs toward center — grouped eggs can be destroyed with one AoE (Detonate)
- **This creates the core decision:** spend attacks on eggs (prevention) or on the Queen (progress)

### Player Counterplay

- **Egg management is mandatory:** If you ignore eggs, you'll be fighting 6 minions + the Queen. Clear eggs before they hatch
- **AoE is king:** Singularity + Detonate destroys clustered eggs and damages the Queen simultaneously
- **Ranged vs melee tradeoff:** Ranged attacks can snipe eggs safely. Melee kills eggs instantly but requires getting close to the Queen (risk of touch damage and Shriek)
- **Card synergies:**
  - **Singularity + Detonate:** Group eggs, destroy them all, Queen takes AoE damage
  - **Chain Lightning:** Bounces between the Queen and her minions for efficient multi-target damage
  - **Corrupt:** DoT ticks on the Queen while you deal with minions
  - **Tag + Execute:** Tag the Queen multiple times while clearing minions, then Execute when minions are dead
  - **Overwhelm:** Surrounded by minions = massive damage bonus. Risky but rewarding
  - **Fury:** Taking minion hits while attacking the Queen scales Fury damage
  - **Convergence Matrix scaffold:** Minions naturally cluster near the Queen, giving +30% damage to grouped enemies — this applies to the Queen too
  - **Decay Protocol scaffold:** Tag or Corrupt the Queen, and she takes passive damage while you manage minions

### Visual Design

- Large, bulbous organic entity with visible egg sacs
- Eggs are translucent spheres with visible larvae inside
- Minions are smaller, paler versions of standard Bugs
- Phase 2: Queen's egg sacs pulse and glow, movement becomes erratic
- Shriek visual: expanding ring of distortion

### Why This Boss Is Unique

No other encounter has **enemy spawning**. The Swarm Queen turns a single-target fight into a multi-target management problem. The player's deck composition matters enormously — an AoE-heavy deck (Singularity, Detonate, Chain Lightning) handles this fight well, while a single-target deck (Phase Shift + Backstab) struggles to keep up with egg production.

## Boss 3: Mirror

*A shape-shifting entity that replicates the player's attacks. The only fight where the player must think about what they're giving the boss.*

### Concept

The Mirror copies the player's deck composition. It uses the same card types the player has equipped, firing mirrored versions back at them. The more diverse and powerful the player's deck, the more versatile the Mirror's attacks. This creates a unique tension: your strongest cards are also your biggest threat.

### Stats

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Base HP | 240 |
| HP at round 5 | 312 (× 1.30) |
| HP at round 10 | 384 (× 1.60) |
| HP at round 15 | 456 (× 1.90) |
| Touch damage | 16 |
| Movement | Matches player's movement pattern — mirrors player position horizontally across arena center (if player is 200px left of center, Mirror is 200px right) |
| Hitbox radius | 35px |

### Phase System

2 phases, transitioning at 50% HP.

**Phase 1: Reflection**
- Mirror copies the player's Statement cards as melee attacks
- Fires mirrored projectiles based on the player's Function cards
- Maintains mirrored positioning

**Phase 2: Refraction**
- Mirror stops mirroring position, becomes aggressive
- Uses combined attacks from the player's deck simultaneously
- Gains a dash ability that matches the player's Dash speed
- Attack speed increases by 40%

### Core Mechanic: Deck Reading

At the start of the boss fight, the Mirror reads the player's current deck composition and builds its attack pattern from it:

| Player's Deck Contains | Mirror Gains |
|---|---|
| Slash | Melee arc attack (23 damage, 166px range, 0.13s lock) |
| Bolt | Projectile attack (30 damage, 590 px/s, 20px hit radius, no siphon) |
| Trim | No attack (Mirror doesn't copy draw effects) |
| Refund | No attack (Mirror doesn't copy discount effects) |
| Phase Shift | Teleport dash (200px, 1.0s cooldown) |
| Backstab | Rear attack that deals 2× damage if player recently attacked (within 0.5s) |
| Expose | Applies Vulnerable to player for 4s (+40% damage taken) |
| Singularity | Gravity well pull (180px radius, 1.0s duration) |
| Detonate | AoE explosion (160px radius, 25 base + 10 per mirror attack active) |
| Shockwave | Push attack (120px knockback on player) |
| Corrupt | DoT projectile (3 damage/tick for 4 ticks, 30% slow) |
| Iterate | Melee damage that ramps each time it attacks (+6 per hit, no cap) |
| Echo | Copies its last attack's damage |
| Fortify | Gains 20 Shield (absorbs damage) |
| Shatter | Melee dealing 10 + 2× Mirror's current Shield |
| Bastion | Consumes all Shield for burst in melee arc |
| Tag | Applies Mark to player (each Mark: +10% damage taken from Mirror, max 3 Marks) |
| Execute | Consumes all Marks on player for bonus damage |
| Spike | Thorns aura: player takes 12 damage on melee contact for 3s |
| Conduit | Melee dealing 8 + 8 per debuff on player |
| Fury | Melee dealing 10 + 4 × (times Mirror has been hit this phase) |
| Chain Lightning | Bouncing projectile (15 damage, 3 bounces, 200px range) |
| Overflow | Projectile dealing 0.4 × Mirror's remaining "compute" (starts at 80, each attack spends ~20) |
| Momentum | Melee (costs Mirror nothing, deals 18 damage) |
| Surge | Projectile (25 damage, small splash) |
| Blood Price | Melee (35 damage, Mirror loses 10% HP per use) |
| Overwhelm | Melee dealing 8 + 5 × (enemies near Mirror — counts player + any remaining minions) |
| Resonance | Projectile that applies last debuff again |
| Precision | Projectile dealing 12 + 0.1 × distance |

**Design intent:** A player running Slash + Bolt + Trim has a simple Mirror. A player running 7 different card types faces a Mirror with 7 different attacks. Your deck IS the difficulty.

### Attack Pattern (Phase 1)

The Mirror cycles through its available attacks in a semi-random pattern:

- **Attack interval:** 2.0s between attacks
- **Each attack:** randomly selected from the Mirror's available attack pool
- **Positioning:** Mirror stays at mirrored position across arena center
- **After each attack:** 0.5s telegraph (white flash) before the next

### Attack Pattern (Phase 2)

- **Attack interval:** 1.2s (40% faster)
- **Mirror stops mirroring position** — now actively chases the player at 120 px/s
- **Combo attacks:** Mirror can use two attacks in rapid succession (0.3s apart) instead of one
- **Dash:** If the player's deck includes Phase Shift or any dash-adjacent card, Mirror gains a Dash (200px, 1.5s cooldown)

### Player Marks (from Tag)

If the Mirror uses Tag and applies Marks to the player:

- Each Mark: +10% damage taken from the Mirror (max 3 Marks = +30%)
- Marks last 6 seconds
- Player can "cleanse" Marks by taking damage (each hit removes 1 Mark)
- Execute consumes all Marks for 10 + 15 × Marks damage

This is the only mechanic where debuffs are applied TO the player, creating a taste of what the player does to enemies.

### Vulnerability Windows

- **After reflected attacks:** The Mirror has a 0.4s recovery after each attack where it moves 50% slower
- **Blood Price penalty:** If the Mirror uses Blood Price, it loses 10% of its current HP. A deck with Blood Price gives the Mirror a self-damaging attack — this is an intentional tradeoff
- **Echo dependency:** If the Mirror's last attack was weak (e.g., a basic Slash it hasn't ramped), Echo copies that weak damage. The player can "feed" the Mirror weak attacks to make Echo harmless

### Player Counterplay

- **Deck awareness:** Know what the Mirror has. A Slash + Bolt + Trim deck faces a Mirror with only 2 attacks (easy to predict). A 7-card-type deck faces a versatile opponent
- **Bait weak attacks:** Let the Mirror use its weakest attacks, then punish during recovery
- **Blood Price is double-edged:** Running Blood Price gives the Mirror a powerful melee attack BUT it also damages the Mirror. Net effect depends on how often the Mirror uses it
- **Anti-synergy exploitation:**
  - Running **Tag** gives the Mirror Marks... but the player can also **Execute** the Mirror. Both sides have Marks, but the player chooses when to Execute
  - Running **Expose** lets the Mirror make the player Vulnerable... but the player can also Expose the Mirror. Whoever applies it first gains the advantage
- **Card synergies:**
  - **Singularity + Detonate:** The Mirror probably has these too, but the player's version is stronger (player's Detonate scales with enemies hit, Mirror's doesn't get the same scaling)
  - **Iterate:** If the Mirror has Iterate, it ramps over the fight. If the player also has Iterate, they're in a ramp race. Kill the Mirror before its Iterate outdamages yours
  - **Fortify + Shatter:** Shield absorbs the Mirror's attacks. Shatter deals bonus damage while Shield holds. The Mirror might also have Shield, making it a war of attrition
  - **Phase Shift:** Teleport behind the Mirror. If the Mirror has Phase Shift, it can teleport away too — creating a teleport-tag fight
  - **Precision Protocol scaffold:** The Mirror stays at range in Phase 1. Perfect for stacking Precision Protocol and dealing bonus Function damage
  - **Diversity Protocol scaffold:** If you have 7+ unique cards, you get +14 damage per attack... but so does the Mirror's versatility. The scaffold makes you stronger AND makes the fight harder
  - **Berserker Protocol scaffold:** Taking hits from the Mirror's varied attacks triggers Berserker. The more attacks the Mirror has, the more Berserker triggers. Chaos favors the reckless

### Visual Design

- A shifting, amorphous humanoid form made of reflective surfaces
- When using a copied attack, briefly takes on visual traits of that attack type (melee arc glow for Statement, projectile energy for Function)
- Phase 1: smooth, mirror-like surface. Mirror image behavior is obvious
- Phase 2: surface cracks and fragments, becomes more aggressive and jagged
- Player Marks: small dark diamonds orbit the player (mirroring the Mark visual on enemies)
- Attack telegraphs: the Mirror "charges" the same way the player's cards animate, but mirrored

### Why This Boss Is Unique

No other encounter reads the player's deck. The Mirror is the only boss where **deck composition directly determines difficulty**. A starter deck (15 Slash + 5 Bolt) faces a Mirror with only 2 attack types — the easiest version. A heavily customized deck with 8+ card types faces a Mirror with a full arsenal — the hardest version. This creates a natural difficulty curve tied to progression, not arbitrary HP bloat.

It also creates replayability: fighting the Mirror with different decks produces entirely different boss encounters.

## Implementation Priority

1. **Architect** — Stationary boss with wall/placement mechanics. No new enemy AI needed (no chase, no pathfinding). Most straightforward to implement. Tests the arena-modification system (walls, zones).
2. **Swarm Queen** — Builds on existing Bug AI (minions use Bug chase/lunge). New systems: enemy spawning during combat, egg objects. Tests multi-target management.
3. **Mirror** — Most complex. Requires deck-reading system, mirrored attack execution, per-card attack implementations. Tests the onEffect system and dynamic damage pipeline. Most unique and replayable.

## Boss Pool & Encounter Rules

### Pool Composition at Launch

3 bosses: Architect, Swarm Queen, Mirror.

### Selection Rules

- Each boss round (5, 10, 15, ...) randomly selects one boss from the full pool
- **No immediate repeats:** The same boss cannot appear twice in a row. If round 5 is Architect, round 10 is randomly selected from {Swarm Queen, Mirror}
- **No other restrictions:** All 3 bosses are available from round 5 onward

### Boss Round Flow

1. Normal enemies spawn and are cleared (same as any round)
2. 1.5s transition: arena darkens, boss entry
3. Boss fight begins
4. Boss death: extraction gate unlocks
5. Player extracts to finish the round

### Credit Rewards

| Outcome | Credits |
|---|---|
| Boss killed + extracted | (normal kills × 12 + 36) + (boss kill bonus: 60 + 10 × roundsFinished) |
| Retreated (boss alive) | normal kills × 8 (no boss bonus) |
| Decommissioned | 0 |

Example round 5: 10 normal kills + boss = (10 × 12 + 36) + (60 + 50) = 156 + 110 = **266 credits**.
